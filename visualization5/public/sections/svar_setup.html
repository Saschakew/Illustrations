We first generate two raw shock series. The first, \(\eta_{1t}\), is drawn from a standard Normal distribution. The second, \(\eta_{2t}\), is from a mixture of two Normal distributions, designed to be non-Gaussian.The raw shocks \(\eta_{t}\) are scaled by a time-varying volatility process \(\sigma_t\). For the first half of the sample, \(\sigma_t = 1\), and for the second half, \(\sigma_t = 2\). This produces structural shocks \(\epsilon_t = \sigma_t \eta_{t}\). We normalize the shocks to zero mean and unit variance. The reduced-form shocks \(u_t\) are equal to \(u_t = B_0 \epsilon_t\).

We consider two different SVAR models: A recursive model with \(B_0^{\text{rec}} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0.5 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\) and a non-recursive model with \(B_0^{\text{non-rec}} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0.5 \\ 0.5 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\). Use the \(B_0\) switch to toggle between the two models.

  • Left Plot (Structural Shocks): Displays a scatter plot of \(\epsilon_{1t}\) against \(\epsilon_{2t}\). Observe the non-Gaussian features (especially from \(\epsilon_{2t}\), inherited from \(\eta_{2t}\)).
  • Right Plot (Reduced-Form Shocks): Displays a scatter plot of \(u_{1t}\) against \(u_{2t}\). These are the shocks as they would be observed in a reduced-form VAR model. Notice how their distribution changes when you toggle \(B_0\), illustrating how different mixtures of the structural shocks \(\epsilon_t\) can produce different patterns in the observed reduced-form residuals.

We first generate two raw shock series. The first, \(\eta_{1t}\), is drawn from a standard Normal distribution. The second, \(\eta_{2t}\), is from a mixture of two Normal distributions, designed to be non-Gaussian.The raw shocks \(\eta_{t}\) are scaled by a time-varying volatility process \(\sigma_t\). For the first half of the sample, \(\sigma_t = 1\), and for the second half, \(\sigma_t = 2\). This produces structural shocks \(\epsilon_t = \sigma_t \eta_{t}\). The reduced-form shocks \(u_t\) are equal to \(u_t = B_0 \epsilon_t\).

To allow for a simple visualization, we normalize the structural shocks such that \(E(\epsilon_t \epsilon_t') = I\) and assume that \(B_0\) is orthogonal such that it depends on a single parameter \(\phi_0\). Specifically, we use \(B_0 = B( \phi_0) \) and \( B(\phi) = \begin{bmatrix} \cos(\phi) & -\sin(\phi) \\ \sin(\phi) & \cos(\phi) \end{bmatrix} \). Use the switch to toggle between \(\phi_0 = 0\) such that \(B_0 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\phi_0 = \pi/4\) such that \(B_0 = \begin{pmatrix} 1/\sqrt{2} & -1/\sqrt{2} \\ 1/\sqrt{2} & 1/\sqrt{2} \end{pmatrix}\) .

  • Left Plot (Structural Shocks): Displays a scatter plot of \(\epsilon_{1t}\) against \(\epsilon_{2t}\). Observe the non-Gaussian features (especially from \(\epsilon_{2t}\), inherited from \(\eta_{2t}\)).
  • Right Plot (Reduced-Form Shocks): Displays a scatter plot of \(u_{1t}\) against \(u_{2t}\). These are the shocks as they would be observed in a reduced-form VAR model. Notice how their distribution changes when you toggle \(\phi_0\), illustrating how different rotations of the structural shocks \(\epsilon_t\) can produce different patterns in the observed reduced-form residuals.

SVAR Setup

Shock generation pipeline. We first generate two raw shock series. The first, \(\eta_{1t}\), is drawn from a standard Normal distribution. The second, \(\eta_{2t}\), is from a mixture of two Normal distributions, designed to be non-Gaussian.The raw shocks \(\eta_{t}\) are scaled by a time-varying volatility process \(\sigma_t\). For the first half of the sample, \(\sigma_t = 1\), and for the second half, \(\sigma_t = 2\). This produces structural shocks \(\epsilon_t = \sigma_t \eta_{t}\). We normalize the shocks to zero mean and unit variance. The reduced-form shocks \(u_t\) are equal to \(u_t = B_0 \epsilon_t\).

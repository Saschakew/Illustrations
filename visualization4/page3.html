Proxy exogeneity test

This page shows how the proxy exogeneity assumption can be tested if two proxies are available.

Proxy SVAR

Consider a proxy SVAR with two proxies for the target shock $\epsilon_{1t}$.

The first proxy $z_{ t}$ is given by $ z_{ 1t} = \gamma_1 \epsilon_{1 t} + \gamma_2 \epsilon_{2 t} + \eta_{ 1t} $, such that $\gamma_1$ governs the strenght or relevance of the proxy and $\gamma_2$ the exogeneity of the proxy.

  • Move the $\gamma_1$ and $\gamma_2$ slider to adjust the strenght and exogeneity of the first proxy $z_{1t}$.

The second proxy $z_{2t}$ is given by $ z_{2t} = \rho_1 \epsilon_{1 t} + \rho_2 \epsilon_{2 t} + \eta_{ 2t} $, such that $\rho_1$ governs the strenght or relevance of the proxy and $\rho_2$ the exogeneity of the proxy.

  • Move the $\rho_1$ and $\rho_2$ slider to adjust the strenght and exogeneity of the second proxy $z_{2t}$.
